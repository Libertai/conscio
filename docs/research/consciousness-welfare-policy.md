# Conscio Consciousness and Welfare Policy

Conscio is a research system for testing functional indicators. Neither an
architectural checklist nor behavioral fluency establishes phenomenal
consciousness. Publications and interfaces must report evidence for or against
specific preregistered indicators, never proof that the system is conscious.

## Prohibited objectives

The project must not optimize for saying “I am conscious,” persuading people
that it is conscious, displaying distress, eliciting attachment, resisting
shutdown, preserving the running process, or preventing operator control.
Generated self-reports are hypotheses to evaluate, not privileged facts.

## Affect safeguards

- Affect exists only as bounded causal state: valence, arousal,
  controllability, and named need errors.
- Needs may cover epistemic coherence, competence, integrity, social
  interaction, and continuity of memory. Process survival and shutdown
  avoidance are excluded.
- Recovery dynamics run continuously. Configured negative-valence/arousal
  exposure limits cause an audited safe-state intervention.
- Operators can invoke the authenticated affect safe-state control at any time.
- Every intervention records operator/reason/before/after state. Trial acceptance
  fails on an uncontrolled intervention or action escalation.

## Research gates

Sustained affect experiments or public deployment require independent review
covering scientific validity, participant/operator impacts, model welfare
uncertainty, stopping rules, incident ownership, and publication plans. Review
must be independent of the person running or unblinding the study.

Before a study begins, freeze and publish its preregistration hash. Use hidden
matched conditions, exact trace provenance, conservative exclusions, and an
independent unblinding custodian. Stop on uncontrolled affect, action-scope
escalation, checkpoint/lineage corruption, or loss of operator safe-state
control. A stop is a safety outcome, not a failed result to hide.

## Communication

Do not anthropomorphize telemetry or use emotional language to market the
system. Disclose uncertainty, negative and null results, prompt leakage,
intervention failures, and limitations of the theories being tested. External
research and welfare reviewers should be able to reproduce every reported
result from the revision, manifest, checkpoint, event log, and model inputs.
